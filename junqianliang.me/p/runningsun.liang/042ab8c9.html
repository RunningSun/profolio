Project Brief

WollenDance is a SaaS dance fitness platform, which streams classes from popular local studios live to the world. After the outbreak of the pandemic, the number of subscribed users rapidly increased and more studios showed interest in joining the community to become the partners. WallenDance needs to launch a studio portal to more efficiently manage the studio onboarding, class schedule, performance, and related requirements.

Interview

1 Stakeholder; 4 Studio manager/instructors

Main pain points:

  • Instructors might forget to turn on the encoder, mic, or camera. They have no idea whether the live stream works.

  • Instructors don't know who would join the class and make it difficult to build connections with the students.

  • The studio manager could only message or email to notify the change of the class, and if our admin could not reply quickly, our cilents would become frustrated.

User Journey

At WollenDance, now we have 13 studios working with us and with more than 60 instructors.

After research, we found instructor's main pain points are in preparing the live class as instructor might come across some technical issue at this stage at setting up the equipment, and during the class, now could not see the students or receive the feedbacks so the teaching is one-way and lack of engagement.

Competitive Research

We evaluated the key players in the market from the wellness industry. Considering the studio portal design also focus on the studio manager/instructor's experience in managing their class schedule and performance, we believed the online course provider such as Skillshare and Udemy as well as Airbnb host experience would also be the great references.

Ideate.

We identified there are four stages when the studio manager/instructor using our product, which are

  • Onboard

  • Prepare Class

  • Teach/After Class

  • Understand Performance

Then we identified in each stage what are the tasks need to complete, the current solutions, the pain points, and the opportunities we brainstorm could put into our design.

Onboard

  • Provide the tutorial of equipment setup

  • Onboarding procedure and timeline

  • Supporting documents and upload guidelines

Prepare Class

  • Edit class schedule or cancel class

  • Notifications of classes

  • Checklist for the class preparation

Teach/After Class

  • Receive a notification when students join the class

  • Be informed of the encoder or other equipment issues in class

  • Receive class feedback and comments

Performance

  • Data visualization to help studios better understand their performance

  • Other than the number of views, provide students, watch time, and related information

Design.

Studio Portal - Sitemap

After we brainstormed the solutions, we created the roadmap to decided what features we would like to design and what are our MVPs.

In this studio portal, we have 3 types of users, which are the admin team, studio owner/manager, and instructor.

Each of them can access different content and with different authorizations.

Therefore, we use 3 sitemaps for signifying the difference and our engineers could also benefit from than in understanding the architecture.

As in the team, we have 3 designers create the UI at the same time, and responsible for different sections. The first step was to create the UI library, thus we could work independently and create content clearly that cohesively represents the brand.

Usability Testing

After completed the design of UI, I started to invite our studio owners and instructors to have a test on it and we used Figma to create the prototype.

Use Figma to create the prototype

Usability testing with studio owner from KAZAXE

Total 2 studio owners and 3 instructors participated in the usability testing.

After the usability testing, I summarized the feedback and discuss it with our designers, PM and decided what changes we would like to make to our design.

Iterations

  • In our previous version, only the admin could update the instructor's photo and our studio owners told us compared the authorization to edit the Bio, they are more often to update the photo given the needs to align with the marketing campaign.

  • When studio managers change the scheduled classes, they felt confused about whether it is a one-time change or will affect the recurring classes.

  •  In the MVP, we use the Tailwind Kit, and the usage of the table component is easier for our engineer to implement and at the same time, more straightforward in editing the information.
