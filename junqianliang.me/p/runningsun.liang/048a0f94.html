Overview

In this 2 month project, I worked with the scheduler team from Civitas Learning to redesign the feature of selecting campus for Ivy Tech Community College, including user flows, wireframes, and UI design.

Client: Civitas Learning is a technology company and they help colleges and universities use data to solve challenges and improve student success. Ivy Tech Community College is one of the colleges using their services and my work in this project is to help Ivy Tech to optimize the website.

+ My Role | Research, UX & UI Design, Prototyping

+ Methods | Competitive Analysis, User Flow, Wireframe, and Visual Design

+ Tools | Figma, Marvel

+ Team | Greg, Chief Architect; Steven, Project Manager; Steve, Developer; Cassie, Manager of Customer Success, Junqian(Me), UX Designer;

+ Time | 8 Weeks

Problem

Ivy Tech Community College has more than 40 campuses located in different places, from the campus in the southernmost to the campus in the northernmost, it takes 4 hours to travel. The college would like to feature in the course schedule to help the student know which campuses are closest to them, rather than registering for classes at a far-away campus that is unable to attend.

Enter address: before selecting the campus, adding the address frame to allow users to enter their address.

List Campus from the closest: show the campus's distance to the student's address and organize the campuses by distance with the closest campus at the top of the list.

Recalculate and save: edit the address and recalculate the distance. Once users save the address they don't need to provide it again when they return.

Research

Competitive Analysis

According to the PM from Civitas Learning, she believes there are no other competitors right now providing this feature. Therefore I decided to start comparing similar features to conduct my analysis. A few keywords: Course scheduler, Campus locator.

The tutorials online to teach the student schedule their courses are good examples to let me understand the whole process, especially in the section of selecting campus, what are the possible features.  

If the campus is not familiar with you, just think of the store locator. It is not a new practice and considering the requirements to organize the campuses based on their distance, the store locator will definitely be a great reference.

Ideate

User Flows

  • User enter the address and the campus list will be ordered by distance with the closest campus at the top of the list

  • User skip address enters and then return to enter the address.

  • User edit address and returning user.

Solution

After confirming the user flows and the wireframes, our team and I have a meeting with our client Ivy Tech Community Technology. In the meeting, as the designer responsible for this feature, I explained my design details. Our client was satisfied with the whole design and they also raised feedback hoping we could add the distance filter. Therefore, in my UI design, I add the filter button to help users select the campus in more ways.

Take away

  1. Involve developers from the start and on a regular basis. My first ideation for the feature is to embed the map as I believe it would provide users a more intuitive understanding of the direction and distance. After discussing with the developer in the early phase, they raise some concerns in data privacy, cost, and deployment. After discussing with the team, we decided to remove it. Let the team members feel included is important to building a good relationship and a good product.

My first ideation of the feature and the developers have concerns such

as data privacy so in the later version the map was not included.

  1. Remote Collaboration. In this time, we have all experienced some sort of zoom/slack call exhaustion due to the pandemic. Adapting yourself to the changes and relying on some tools to well organize the meetings and ensure to show up on time. Line up with the team, be honest and open when giving or receiving feedback, and be concise and clear especially because the other person can't always be right next to you.

Meeting with the scheduler team to walk through the product.

  1. Find Impact. No matter what the scale of the project, always look into the big picture, how does your design fit into the picture in relation to the product? When clients purpose different ideas about the project, how to use your knowledge to help them to prioritize the tasks. Always keep in mind you are the voice of the user, put users first.
